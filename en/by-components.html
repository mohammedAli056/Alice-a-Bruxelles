The History of the Schools in 2 Videos

The Baroness and the Barefoot Children

Alice Hallgarten Franchetti: Philanthropy, Women, Schools

Alice Hallgarten—a New Yorker by birth who married Baron Leopoldo Franchetti—divided her years in Italy between Rome and Città di Castello, dedicating herself to improving the living conditions of underserved rural and urban communities. Her practical feminism, combined with visionary philanthropy, left a lasting legacy both locally and internationally. In Rome, she took part in the activities of the Unione di San Lorenzo, engaging in social initiatives in the same neighborhood where Maria Montessori then opened her first Casa dei Bambini. In Città di Castello, she founded the Montesca and Rovigliano Rural Schools, established in 1901 and 1902, to provide education for the children of her farmers. Furthermore, she opened Tela Umbra, a weaving workshop still active today, complete with a daycare center where women could have their children taken care of, while at work.

The Montesca School at the 1910 Brussels World Fair

Alice Hallgarten Franchetti between Maria Montessori and Giuseppe Lombardo Radice

Giuseppe Lombardo Radice wrote of Alice Hallgarten Franchetti that "she worked on her school as if she were responsible for solving the educational problem of every school." In her rural institutions at Montesca and Rovigliano, she gave farmers' children access to globally sourced teaching materials and to the most innovative pedagogical methods of the time. From these schools, educational theorist Giuseppe Lombardo Radice derived the model for the "Calendario della Montesca" and incorporated it into Italy's national curriculum. The Franchettis also inspired Maria Montessori to put her Scientific Pedagogy Method to paper. In 1909, she was their guest at Villa Montesca where they hosted the inauguration of the first course for training teachers in the Montessori Method—led by the Dottoressa herself and held in one of their properties in the nearby town.

In 1910, the schools were awarded a Diplôme d'honneur and a gold medal at the Brussels International and Universal Exposition—a formal testament to Baroness Alice Hallgarten Franchetti's tireless dedication.

Historical Reproduction

This carefully crafted reproduction captures the essence of the original display, preserving the educational and historical significance of the collection for contemporary viewers.

Reproduction Details

  • Based on: 1910 photograph
  • Realized by: Giuseppina Benedetti
  • Year: 2024
  • Type: Historical reconstruction

Evergreen Leaves Collection

Display case containing a collection of 'Evergreen leaves'. This piece represents the botanical studies conducted at the school, showcasing the diversity of local flora through carefully preserved specimens.

Collection Details

  • Institution: Tela Umbra Museum
  • Created: 1948–1949
  • Type: Botanical specimen collection
  • Note: Probably a copy of the original

Seeds Dispersed by Animals

Display case containing 'Seeds dispersed by animals'. This educational exhibit demonstrates the various mechanisms by which animals contribute to plant reproduction and seed distribution in nature.

Collection Details

  • Reconstruction by: Giuseppina Benedetti
  • Year: 2024
  • Institution: Tela Umbra Museum
  • Type: Educational botanical display

The Cedar

Display case titled 'The Cedar'. This botanical collection focuses on cedar specimens, showcasing the characteristics and features of this significant tree species in Italian flora.

Collection Details

  • Created: 1924–25
  • Type: Botanical specimen display
  • Note: Copy of the original
  • Focus: Cedar tree specimens and parts

Map of Umbria

A handcrafted geographical map of the Umbria region, created as part of the school's educational curriculum. This three-dimensional relief map demonstrates the topographical features of the local region.

Artwork Details

  • Medium: Papier-mâché on wooden backing
  • Created: At school
  • Type: Educational geography project
  • Subject: Umbria region topography

Annual Ascension Excursion Map

Map of the Monte Santa Maria trip, titled 'The Annual Excursion for the Feast of the Ascension'. This student work documents a school expedition, representing the integration of religious observance with educational outdoor activities.

Artwork Details

  • Student Artist: Vincenzo Pieggi
  • Grade: Class VI (Grade 6)
  • Year Created: 1909
  • Type: Student cartographic work
